# 1. Architectural Philosophy

Buildings are anchors.

Players navigate by architecture long before they memorize maps.

Every building should answer:

- Who uses it?
- Why is it here?
- Why this orientation?
- How is it reached?
- What space does it define?

---

## Rule ARCH-001 — Every Building Defines Space

Buildings should create outdoor rooms rather than simply occupy empty tiles.

Severity: Critical

---

## Rule ARCH-002 — Architecture Supports Navigation

A player should be able to use major buildings as mental landmarks.

Severity: High

# 3. Property Grammar

A property consists of:

- frontage
- entrance
- transition zone
- building
- optional side yard
- optional rear yard
- boundary

---

## Rule PROP-001

Every property must have a readable front.

---

## Rule PROP-002

Buildings should not touch unrelated objects without an intentional transition.

Examples of valid transitions:

- path
- fence
- hedge
- retaining wall
- garden
- foundation

---

# 4. Entrances

The entrance is the most important architectural feature.

---

## Rule ENTRY-001

Every entrance must be visible from its approach.

Critical

---

## Rule ENTRY-002

Every entrance connects directly to navigable terrain.

Critical

---

## Rule ENTRY-003

Do not obscure entrances with trees, signs, NPCs or decoration.

High

## Rule HOME-001

Neighborhoods should vary spacing while preserving overall order.

---

# 7. Town Composition

A town should read as one coherent composition.

Primary components:

- central landmark
- circulation
- districts
- visual anchors
- open space
- edges

---

## Rule TOWN-001

Every town should possess one unmistakable focal point.

---

## Rule TOWN-002

The player should understand the town layout within one minute.

---

## Rule TOWN-003

Do not distribute major buildings evenly.

Hierarchy requires imbalance.

# 8. Landmarks

Landmarks improve memory.

Good landmarks are:

- unique
- visible
- stable
- meaningful

Examples:

- windmill
- giant tree
- fountain
- statue
- tower
- arena

---

## Rule LAND-001

Every settlement should contain at least one unique landmark.

---

## Rule LAND-002

Important landmarks should be visible before the player reaches them.

# 9. Streets & Plazas

Open areas are intentional.

Plazas provide:

- orientation
- emotional relief
- NPC gathering
- event space

---

## Rule STREET-001

Roads connect destinations.

They should never wander without purpose.

---

## Rule STREET-002

Large open spaces require a visual anchor.

Examples:

- fountain
- statue
- garden
- monument

# 10. Architectural Failure Catalog

## AF-001 Sticker Building

Building appears pasted onto terrain.

Fix:
Resolve foundation and surrounding ground.

---

## AF-002 Hidden Door

Entrance obscured.

Fix:
Increase approach clarity.

---

## AF-003 Grid Neighborhood

Every building equally spaced.

Fix:
Introduce controlled asymmetry.

---

## AF-004 Landmark Competition

Multiple buildings compete equally.

Fix:
Establish hierarchy.

---

## AF-005 Decorative Overload

Decoration obscures architecture.

Fix:
Reduce props before moving buildings.

---

# 11. AI Building Audit

Evaluate every building.

Questions:

- Is it the correct hierarchy?
- Is its entrance obvious?
- Does terrain respond to it?
- Does it create space?
- Is it memorable?
- Is its approach readable?
- Does it strengthen navigation?

Output:

```markdown
# Architecture Audit

Critical Issues
High Issues
Medium Issues

Revision Order

Do NOT:
- redraw assets
- increase detail
- add decoration
- replace sprites

Only improve relationships.
```

---

# Final Principle

A player should remember **where** a building was long before they remember **what** it looked like.

Architecture is spatial design first and visual design second.
