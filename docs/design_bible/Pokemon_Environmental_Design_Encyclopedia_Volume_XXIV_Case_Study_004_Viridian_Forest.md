# 1. Scope

Viridian Forest introduces players to a confined natural environment.

Core responsibilities:

- transition from open routes to enclosed spaces,
- teach maze navigation,
- establish environmental atmosphere,
- increase encounter complexity without overwhelming the player.

---

# 2. Dungeon Role

## Observation

The forest is substantially more enclosed than the areas before it.

## Interpretation

The contrast with Route 1 increases the perceived scale of the world.

## Transferable Lesson

A player's first dungeon should emphasize a change in environment before a dramatic increase in difficulty.

---

# 3. Entrance Design

Observation:

- Clear threshold.
- Distinct identity from surrounding routes.
- Immediate reduction in visibility.

Interpretation:

Crossing the entrance should feel like entering a different space, not simply another route.

- PAT-603 Forest Mouth

# 4. Navigation Structure

Expected characteristics:

- Multiple intersections.
- Controlled uncertainty.
- Strong environmental boundaries.
- Frequent reorientation opportunities.

# 5. Visual Composition

Observation:

Trees create the dominant spatial frame.

Interpretation:

The environment relies on enclosure rather than decorative density to communicate identity.

Transferable Lesson:

Large terrain masses are more effective than scattered props for creating atmosphere.

---

# 6. Encounter Ecology

Observation:

Tall grass, paths, and clearings work together to regulate encounter rhythm.

Interpretation:

Combat pacing is shaped by environment rather than encounter tables alone.

Future measurements:

- Grass coverage.
- Safe-area ratio.
- Average distance between encounter zones.

# 7. Pacing

Entrance
→ Orientation
→ Exploration
→ Trainer
→ Clearing
→ Exploration
→ Exit

Alternation between compression and relief should be measured during atlas extraction.

# 10. Transferable Lessons

When designing an introductory forest:

- Use enclosure instead of clutter.
- Create memorable clearings.
- Balance uncertainty with reorientation.
- Let terrain control pacing.
- Keep exits legible once discovered.
